Why Agents Emerged

The deck starts with the market moment, then shows why a single giant prompt was an intuitive but limited first move.

Study

The opening argument is about structure, not hype

The presentation frames agentic AI as mainstream, then narrows to one concrete use case: customer support in 2024, when ChatGPT was popular and APIs were mature enough to build with. The first instinct was a big monolith: one "grand prompt" expected to cover status requests, complaints, happy-customer moments, polite small talk, and even handoff to human agents.

  • The speaker distinguishes two broad styles early: workflow agents and autonomous agents.
  • This deck intentionally focuses on workflow agents and defers autonomous agents to later.
  • The monolith slide is visual but important: it shows one prompt being stretched across multiple support intents.
  • The motivating problem is not abstract research; it is a practical support system that must handle different user needs.
